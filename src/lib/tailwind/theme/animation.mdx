## [Transition Timing Function](https://tailwindcss.com/docs/transition-timing-function)
Specifies the speed curve of the transition effect. <small>[MDN Transition Timing Function](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/transition-timing-function)</small>
<Source language="js" code={`transitionTimingFunction: ${JSON.stringify(component.transitionTimingFunction, null, 2)}`} />

### DEFAULT
This is the default easing function, commonly known as "ease" in CSS. It creates a smooth transition that starts slowly, speeds up in the middle, and then slows down again towards the end.
<Source language="js" code={`DEFAULT: ${JSON.stringify(component.transitionTimingFunction.DEFAULT, null, 2)}`} />
- **0.4, 0**: Controls the initial portion of the transition, making it start slowly.
- **0.2, 1**: Controls the end portion of the transition, making it slow down towards the end.

### linear
The `linear` easing function creates a transition that occurs at a constant speed from start to finish. There are no accelerations or decelerations.
<Source language="js" code={`linear: ${JSON.stringify(component.transitionTimingFunction.linear, null, 2)}`} />
- This means the change happens at the same rate throughout the duration, resulting in a straight line when plotted on a graph.

### in
This is the "ease-in" function, which starts slowly and then speeds up.
<Source language="js" code={`in: ${JSON.stringify(component.transitionTimingFunction.in, null, 2)}`} />
- **0.4, 0**: Controls the initial portion of the transition, making it start slowly.
- **1, 1**: Controls the end portion, making it finish quickly.

### out
This is the "ease-out" function, which starts quickly and then slows down towards the end.
<Source language="js" code={`out: ${JSON.stringify(component.transitionTimingFunction.out, null, 2)}`} />
- **0, 0**: Controls the initial portion of the transition, making it start quickly.
- **0.2, 1**: Controls the end portion, making it slow down towards the end.

### in-out
This is the "ease-in-out" function, which starts slowly, speeds up in the middle, and then slows down again at the end.
<Source language="js" code={`'in-out': ${JSON.stringify(component.transitionTimingFunction['in-out'], null, 2)}`} />
- **0.4, 0**: Controls the initial portion of the transition, making it start slowly.
- **0.2, 1**: Controls the end portion of the transition, making it slow down towards the end.
